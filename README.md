# Kakuma & Kalobeyei Water Access Analysis Project : A Data Analytics Capstone Project
1. Project Title

Kakuma & Kalobeyei Water Access and Sanitation Data Analysis

2. Business Problem

Humanitarian agencies in Kakuma and Kalobeyei need reliable, data-driven insights on water access, sanitation conditions, and distance to water points to improve resource allocation, planning, and monitoring.

However, available field data is scattered and not analyzed in a centralized, decision-ready format.

3. Business Understanding

Water scarcity affects:

Daily household survival

Time spent collecting water

Health and sanitation

Overall humanitarian well-being

This project provides actionable insights for NGOs, WASH partners, and decision-makers.

8. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Summary

Key insights:

0% of households meet the 20L/person/day Sphere Standard

Kalobeyei, Kakuma 1 & Kakuma 2 have the worst sanitation scores

Distance to water sources is far above recommended levels

Boreholes are the most common but least stable sources

9. Conclusions

Water access is far below humanitarian standards

Poor sanitation correlates directly with poor water access

Zones require targeted interventions and monitoring

Dashboard visualizations support real-time decision making
